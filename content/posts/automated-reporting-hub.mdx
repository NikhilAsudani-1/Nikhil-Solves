Every Monday morning, the same four reports. Pull the data, clean it, paste it into Excel, format it, email it. Three hours gone before the week even started.

So I built the thing that should have existed already.

## The Setup

The reports lived in Snowflake. The logic was SQL. The formatting was pure manual suffering.

I used Snowflake's Streamlit-in-Snowflake feature to build a web app that lives directly in the data warehouse — no external hosting, no credentials to manage, no "wait for IT."

## What It Does

One button per report. Click it, get a formatted Excel file. That's it.

Under the hood: parameterized SQL queries, `lxml` for Excel formatting, and a date-range picker so anyone on the team can pull historical data without bugging an analyst.

## The Result

12 hours saved per week. Across a year, that's 600 hours. That's 15 full work weeks.

The boring math is always the most convincing argument.
